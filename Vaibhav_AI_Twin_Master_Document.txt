Internship Deep-Dives
Product Strategy & Operations Intern | Pinch Lifestyle Services Pvt. Ltd. (June 2025 – Dec 2025)
About Pinch: Pinch Lifestyle Services is a premium concierge company that assigns a dedicated Lifestyle Manager (LM) to each client. The LM handles all tasks on the client's behalf — managing errands, travel, purchases, event planning, and expenses — acting as a personal assistant for high-net-worth individuals.
My Role: I worked as a Product Strategy & Operations Intern with dual ownership — internal tooling for the operations team and client-facing product improvements. I wore multiple hats: product manager, operations analyst, and data strategist.
Key Projects & Contributions:
1. Finance Reconciliation Application
Before I joined, the finance team was managing expense reconciliation entirely on manual Excel sheets. For 80 clients, this process took 10 days every cycle — error-prone, slow, and completely unscalable. I identified this as a critical operational bottleneck and took ownership of solving it end-to-end.
I conducted user research with the finance team to understand their exact pain points, mapped out the ideal workflow, and authored detailed PRDs for a purpose-built reconciliation application. The app integrated directly with the company's payments provider and invoice generation system, and featured a direct mail system so the finance team could send payment reminders, resolve invoice disputes, and communicate with clients — all from one place.
The result: reconciliation time dropped from 10 days for 80 clients to 3 days for 120 clients — a 300% improvement in finance team efficiency. More importantly, it eliminated manual errors and gave the finance team visibility and control they never had before.
2. HarmonyOS Internal Tool — OCR Feature for Lifestyle Managers
HarmonyOS is Pinch's internal operations platform used by all 25 Lifestyle Managers to manage client tasks, track expenses, and handle payments made on clients' behalf. A major friction point was task creation — LMs had to manually enter every detail from bills and invoices, which was tedious and time-consuming.
I identified this inefficiency and worked with the tech team to implement OCR (Optical Character Recognition) technology directly into the task creation flow. LMs could now simply upload a bill or invoice photo and the system would auto-populate all relevant fields — no manual entry needed. I contributed by defining the feature requirements, mapping the user journey, and writing the PRD.
This single feature doubled the client-handling capacity of Lifestyle Managers — from managing X clients to 2X — because they spent significantly less time on administrative data entry and more time on high-value client interactions.
3. Churn Analysis & Reduction Strategy
When I joined, the client churn rate stood at 20% — a serious problem for a subscription-based concierge service. I led a comprehensive churn analysis initiative to understand why clients were leaving and what could be done to retain them.
The analysis drew on multiple data signals: transaction history, WhatsApp chat behavior patterns, task frequency, and task severity (i.e., whether clients were trusting LMs with high-stakes tasks like travel or medical appointments, which indicates deeper dependency on the service). From this multi-dimensional dataset, I built a churn scoring framework that could predict — up to one month in advance — which clients were at high risk of churning. This gave the team a proactive window to intervene with personalized outreach and attention.
On the product side, I contributed to strategy decisions that emerged from the analysis: launching festive plans tied to seasonal events, introducing occasional freebies, and creating engagement triggers to make clients more dependent on the service in high-frequency categories. Over 5 months, churn dropped from 20% to 5% — a 75% reduction.
